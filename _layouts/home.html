Ian Abraham

{{ content }} I am a PhD candidate in robotics at the Center for Robotics and Biosystems at Northwestern University where I work on optimal sensing and control with Todd D. Murphey.

During my Ph.D. time at Northwestern, I interned at the NVIDIA Seattle Robotics Lab where I worked on model-based control for kitchen robotics. In addition, I was a part of the DARPA FX-3 Urban Swarm Challenge where I was able to implement my decentralized ergodic control work on a swarm of field robots.

Research

I am interested in a lot of different aspects of robotics (sensing, learning, control, swarm coordination, imitation and adaptation). As a result, my research often resides at the intersection of many different subfields within robotics. In general, my research aim is to enable robotic systems to be self-sufficient and adaptive in the field with minimal human intervention.
